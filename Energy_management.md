### Question 2: When do you find yourself in the Stress quadrant?


- I usually feel stressed when I have deadlines, exams, or too many tasks at once. Learning something completely new can also make me stressed at first. Sometimes I feel stressed when I overthink about the future or when things don’t go as planned. But I also know that a little stress pushes me to improve and do better

---

### Question 3: How do you know you are in the Excitement quadrant?


- I feel excited when I’m working on something I really enjoy or when I achieve a goal I’ve been working toward. My energy feels high, and I feel motivated and focused. Time passes quickly, and I don’t feel tired easily. I feel positive, confident, and eager to keep going.

### Question 4: Paraphrase – Sleep is Your Superpower

- Sleep improves brain performance  
- Sleep strengthens memory and learning  
- Lack of sleep reduces focus  
- Sleep boosts immunity  
- Poor sleep increases disease risk  
- Sleep affects mood and mental health  
- Deep sleep restores the body  
- REM sleep supports emotional balance  
---

### Question 5: Ideas to Sleep Better

- Maintain a fixed sleep schedule  
- Avoid screens before bed  
- Reduce bright light at night  
- Get sunlight during the day  
- Avoid caffeine in the evening  
- Keep the room cool and dark  
---

### Question 6: Paraphrase – Brain Changing Benefits of Exercise (Only Points)

- Improves memory  
- Increases focus  
- Boosts mood  
- Reduces stress and anxiety  
- Promotes brain cell growth  
- Protects against brain aging

### Question 7: Steps to Exercise More

- Start with small daily goals  
- Fix a consistent workout time  
- Choose enjoyable activities  
- Take stairs instead of lifts  
- Go for short walking breaks  
- Exercise with a friend
