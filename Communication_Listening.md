# Communication & Listening – Notes and Answers

## Question 1 – What are the steps/strategies for Active Listening?

1. Pay complete attention to the speaker.
2. Allow the speaker to finish without interruptions.
3. Concentrate only on what is being discussed.
4. Show engagement through short verbal responses
---

## Question 2 – According to Fisher’s Model, what are the key aspects of Reflective Listening?

1. Carefully receive the speaker’s message.
2. Observe emotions and feelings behind the words.
3. Paraphrase the message in your own language.
4. Confirm understanding by asking questions.
---

## Question 3 – What are the obstacles in your listening process?

1. External distractions like noise or mobile phones.
2. Interrupting before others complete their thoughts.
3. Making assumptions or judging too quickly.
4. Losing concentration during conversations.
---

##  Question 4 – What can you do to improve your listening skills?

1. Give undivided attention to the speaker.
2. Reduce or remove distractions.
3. Practice patience and avoid interruptions.
4. Ask relevant and clear questions.
---

## Question 5 – When do you use a Passive communication style in daily life?

1. To prevent conflicts.
2. When feeling shy or unsure.
3. While speaking with seniors or authority figures.
4. When confidence is low.
---

## Question 6 – When do you use an Aggressive communication style in daily life?

1. During moments of extreme anger.
2. When feeling insulted or disrespected.
3. Under intense pressure or stress.
4. In heated arguments.
---

## Question 7 – When do you use Passive-Aggressive communication styles in daily life?

1. When angry but avoiding direct expression.
2. When feeling ignored or emotionally hurt.
3. To escape direct confrontation.
4. When unsure how to express emotions openly.
---

## Question 8 – How can you make your communication more Assertive?

1. Express thoughts and feelings honestly.
2. Use “I” statements instead of blaming others.
3. Respect both your needs and others’ opinions.
4. Learn to refuse politely but firmly.
